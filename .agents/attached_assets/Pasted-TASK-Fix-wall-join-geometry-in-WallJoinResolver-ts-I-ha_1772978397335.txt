TASK: Fix wall join geometry in WallJoinResolver.ts
I have a BIM application using Three.js. Walls are rendered as BoxGeometry meshes aligned along a baseLine (start/end Vector3 pair). The WallJoinResolver.resolveLevel() function adjusts wall endpoints when walls meet at corners.
The builder works like this:

Each wall is a BoxGeometry(wallLength, height, thickness)
The box is centered on the midpoint of baseLine[0] → baseLine[1]
The box is rotated to face along the wall direction
The box end faces are always perpendicular to the wall direction — no slanting is possible
wallLength = baseLine[0].distanceTo(baseLine[1])

What I need:
When two walls meet at a corner (L-join), both wall endpoints should be moved so the boxes visually overlap and fill the corner with no gap and no exposed end face.
The correct geometry for a BoxGeometry L-join:
Given two walls A and B meeting at corner point I:

tA = unit vector pointing FROM wall A's endpoint INTO wall A's body (inward tangent)
tB = unit vector pointing FROM wall B's endpoint INTO wall B's body (inward tangent)
sinθ = |tA × tB| (cross product magnitude = sine of angle between walls)
T = wall thickness (assume equal for both = 0.2m)

The fix: Move each wall's endpoint from I to:
newEndpointA = I + (T / (2 * sinθ)) * tA
newEndpointB = I + (T / (2 * sinθ)) * tB
This extends each wall past I into the corner, so the two boxes overlap. The overlap is hidden inside the geometry. The outer faces of both boxes meet cleanly at the corner with no gap.
