Solutions Dropdown — PRYZM Targeting Strategy

The two reference examples use different strategies: **Figma** targets by use case + role + org type (broad horizontal platform). **Lovable** targets by job title alone (narrow, transactional). PRYZM sits in between — a specialist platform, but one that serves meaningfully different people in different ways.

The right structure for PRYZM is **two columns**: who the person is, and what workflow they're trying to solve. This matches the actual split in your plan architecture (`architect` → solo; `studio` → team; `firm` → org) and in your feature set (AI tools vs. IFC compliance vs. collaboration).

---

One item NOT to include

"Enterprise" as a standalone Solutions item — it will be confusing. Your nav already has or will have a direct "Pricing" link where Enterprise appears. Put enterprise scale under "Established Practices" and bespoke deployment under "Bespoke Platform Deployment." Don't duplicate.

---

Visual format recommendation

Use the **Lovable pattern** (dark, two columns, bold labels, no category headers) rather than the Figma pattern (light, three columns, category headers). Reasons:

- PRYZM's palette is dark violet — Lovable's dark flyout matches this; Figma's white mega-menu does not
- You have 7 + 7 items — two clean columns, no need for three
- Category headers ("USE CASES", "ROLES") add cognitive load that only pays off if you have 10+ items per column like Figma does

The flyout should sit below the nav bar with a subtle backdrop-blur, `var(--app-surface)` background, `var(--app-radius-lg)` corners, and items in `var(--app-text)` that shift to `var(--app-violet-1)` on hover. That aligns exactly with your existing AppTheme design tokens.
