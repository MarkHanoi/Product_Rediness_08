PRYZM — Deep Monetization Strategy & Execution Plan

Part 1: What You Actually Have (Product Audit)

Before pricing anything, it's critical to understand what you've built, because it's significantly more than a "BIM tool" — and underpricing it would be a strategic mistake.

**Core BIM Engine:**

Walls (single, polyline, curved, arcs, mixed, ortho, by-slab), Slabs with openings, Curtain walls (grid-based, configurable mullions, panel types), Doors & Windows (auto-cut hosted elements with Fire Rating, sill height, frame/leaf colors), Parametric Stairs (I, L, U shapes), Roofs, Parametric Furniture (beds, dining sets, wardrobes with dedicated layout engine), Beams & Columns, Load Path Graph (structural analysis), Levels & Grids, Linear Dimensioning.

**AI Layer (your primary differentiator):**

- Claude Vision API — 4-step wizard that processes a reference photo + text description, generates a 3D proposal, routes to an Approval Store requiring human confirmation before any model mutation
- AI Design Advisor — semantic model analysis, compliance/pattern detection, intent-based suggestions
- Floor Plan AI — extracts wall regions and semantic data directly from uploaded PDF or image floor plans
- AI Wardrobe Factory — specialized generative system for complex wardrobe configurations

**Geospatial (your secondary differentiator):**

CesiumJS bridge synchronized with Three.js, WGS84 georeferencing, GLB export placed live on the globe — "BIM on Earth." No direct competitor at this price tier has this.

**Collaboration & Data:**

Real-time collaboration via Socket.io, version history with snapshots, Supabase-backed persistence (when configured), AI Proposal/Approval audit trail, IFC export, GLB/GLTF export.

**UX Infrastructure:**

Ribbon bar (Revit-like), Property Inspector, Spatial Tree, Project Browser, Schedule Panel, Snapping Manager, Wall Join Resolver, Undo/Redo with full store snapshots.

**Verdict:** This is not a conceptual tool. This is a full-lifecycle BIM platform with AI at its core and geospatial capabilities no competitor at your planned price point offers. It sits between ArchiCAD Studio ($78/mo) and ArchiCAD Collaborate ($225/mo) in capability, and above Snaptrude ($49/mo) in depth.

**Competitive landscape:**

**Buyer behavior:** The AEC purchase cycle is 8–16 weeks for mid-market. Junior architects discover and advocate, principals approve. 95% of buyers decide before contacting sales. Self-service onboarding and transparent pricing matter enormously. Free access to your tool is your sales team for this demographic.

**B2B freemium conversion:** 2–5% baseline, 8–15% for top performers with strong onboarding. Professional mid-market ($1K–$5K ACV) averages ~10% conversion. Your target.

Part 4: Recommended Monetization Model

The Model: Freemium + Tiered Subscription + AI Add-ons

**Why freemium works here specifically:**

1. BIM adoption inside a firm almost always starts with one advocate. Give them free access and they pull in the team.
2. Your natural paywalls (IFC export, AI, Cesium) are the moments of professional use — they don't occur in casual exploration.
3. Web-based with no installation removes friction entirely.
4. Free tier is your sales motion for the 8–16 week firm purchasing cycle.

Target: Students, early evaluators, advocates within firms starting the sales cycle.

What's included:

- Up to 3 saved projects

Part 5: Go-To-Market Phases

**Phase 1 — Validation (Months 1–3)**

- Launch with Free + Architect tiers only
- Goal: 500 free users, 25 paying Architect subscribers = $1,475 MRR
- Measure: AI action usage patterns, which features drive conversion, where users hit limits
- Avoid building Studio/Firm tiers until you know what features matter to teams

**Phase 2 — Team Growth (Months 4–8)**

- Introduce Studio tier
- Build referral mechanic: firms share project links → collaborators see the product → viral loop
- Target: 150 Architect + 20 Studio subscribers = ~$11,850 MRR
- Begin content for firm decision-makers: ROI calculators, IFC compliance docs, case studies

**Phase 3 — Firm Sales (Months 9–18)**

- Introduce Firm tier
- Begin outbound: target 10–50 person architecture firms, specifically those on Revit looking for cost reduction
- Build educational program, partner with architecture schools
- Target: 200 Architect + 40 Studio + 10 Firm = ~~$21,290 MRR (~~$255K ARR)
- Pursue first 2–3 Enterprise contracts

**Phase 4 — Enterprise (Month 18+)**

- Series A territory if growth trajectory holds
- Focus on large firm deals, government sector (IFC compliance is a procurement requirement in many EU markets)
- White-label partnerships with BIM consultancies

Part 6: Key Strategic Decisions

**1. Price in USD globally, but offer regional purchasing power parity for emerging markets.** AEC is booming in Southeast Asia, Latin America, and India. A 40% discount for those markets is still profitable and captures enormous volume.

**2. Annual billing as default, monthly as premium.** Show annual pricing first. Monthly is available at a 20% premium (not a 17% discount — framing matters). This drives cash flow and reduces churn.

**3. The IFC export paywall is non-negotiable.** It is the single clearest professional signal in your product. Anyone who needs IFC output is working on a real project and will pay. Do not include it in Free.

**4. Seed the Cesium/geospatial feature visually on the landing page.** It is your most visually spectacular differentiator and no competitor at your price point has it. Use it in every marketing touchpoint.

**5. The AI Approval Store is a compliance feature waiting to be sold.** Large firms and government clients need audit trails of AI-suggested changes. This is an Enterprise selling point — "AI that you control, with full audit history."

**6. Do not compete on Revit integration first.** Snaptrude leads with "export to Revit." That positions you as a Revit accessory. Position PRYZM as the platform — Revit is legacy infrastructure you can optionally hand off to.
