State: The SlabWallConnectivityService (src/services/SlabWallConnectivityService.ts) correctly extends/shortens the two walls adjacent to a user-moved wall (the "prev" and "next" walls in the slab sketch loop). However, the moved wall itself is not being trimmed or extended to reach those same corners.

Bug: In propagateForSlab(), two corners are computed:

corner_prev_curr = intersection of prevWall's line ∩ movedWall's new line
corner_curr_next = intersection of movedWall's new line ∩ nextWall's line
These corners are used to snap the prev/next wall endpoints — but the moved wall's own baseLine endpoints are never updated to these same corners. Revit-style behavior requires that the moved wall is also trimmed/extended so that:

whichever end of the moved wall is closest to corner_prev_curr → snap that end to corner_prev_curr
the other end → snap to corner_curr_next
Fix location: src/services/SlabWallConnectivityService.ts, inside propagateForSlab(), after both corners are computed. Read both corners (not just one at a time), compute the moved wall's new full baseLine, then call wallStore.update(movedWallId, { baseLine: [...] }). This is safe inside the propagating = true block — the reentrancy guard prevents re-entry, and isJoinResolving() will be false (we're not inside WallJoinResolver's miter pass).
