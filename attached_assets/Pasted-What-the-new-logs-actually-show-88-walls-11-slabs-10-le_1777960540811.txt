The real fix for the 6,644ms LONGTASK:
The prewarm calls renderPipelineManager.render(0) once. But the production pipeline has 4 phases — ScenePass MRT, SSGI, TRAA, Outlines. Each phase is a separate WebGPU render pipeline. The prewarm compiles PSOs for phase 1 only (the forward pass), but phases 3 and 4 (SSGI denoiser + outlines) have their own PSO variants that don't get compiled until they first run.
The fix is to call render(0) multiple times during prewarm — once per pipeline phase — or to force all 4 phases to execute during prewarm. Looking at RenderPipelineManager, the phases activate progressively. You need to force a full multi-pass render during prewarm, not just one render call.
In CreateCurtainWallsOnAllSlabsCommand._prewarmCurtainWallShaders(), change:
typescript// BEFORE — only compiles phase 1 PSOs
window.renderPipelineManager.render(0);

// AFTER — force all pipeline phases to compile their PSOs
// Run multiple render passes to ensure SSGI + outline PSOs are compiled
for (let i = 0; i < 3; i++) {
    window.renderPipelineManager.render(0);
}
Three passes covers: ScenePass MRT (pass 1), SSGI+denoise (pass 2), outlines (pass 3). The cost is 3× the current 151ms prewarm = ~450ms, but it eliminates the 6,644ms post-batch LONGTASK. Net saving: ~6.2 seconds.
